# FRACAS & RAMS Management System - Product Requirements Document

## 1. Application Overview

### 1.1 Application Name
FRACAS & RAMS Management System for KMRC RS(3R) Rolling Stock

### 1.2 Application Description
A comprehensive web-based Failure Reporting and Corrective Action System (FRACAS) integrated with Reliability, Availability, Maintainability and Safety (RAMS) calculation engine for BEML's KMRC RS(3R) metro rolling stock project. The system manages job cards, NCR reports, vendor information, fleet management, maintenance scheduling, and generates RAMS compliance reports including MDBF, MDBCF, MTTR, Availability and Pattern Failure analysis based on EN 50126:1999 standards and KMRC RS(3R) RAMS Plan (DRCA No. RS(3R)/PP/F/B864/A1).

### 1.3 Target Users
- Admin: Full read/write/edit access (Depot Incharge)
- Engineers: Read/write access
- Data Entry Operators: Read/write access
- Officers: Read/write access

### 2.5 RAMS Calculation Engine

#### 2.5.1 Calculation Formulas (Based on RAMS Plan Document DRCA No. RS(3R)/PP/F/B864/A1)

**Failure Classification Logic:**
- **Relevant Failure**: Fault within design/environmental specification limits; improper operation due to contractor documentation; transient failures including No Fault Found (if attributable to rolling stock per Engineer's opinion)
- **Non-Relevant Failure**: Caused by non-contractor equipment; human error (except documentation-related); accidents not associated with normal operation; operation outside design/environmental limits
- **Service Failure**: Relevant failure(s) during revenue service causing: (a) Non-availability to start revenue service after pre-departure checkout, (b) Withdrawal from revenue service, (c) Delay ≥3 minutes

**Availability Calculation:**
```
Percentage Availability = [1 - (DT(OPM) + DT(CM)) / Total Time] × 100
```
Where:
- Total Time = Assessment period hours × Number of commissioned trains
- DT(OPM) = Down Time due to Other Preventive Maintenance (hours) - Total downtime for PM other than service checks, counted from train becoming unfit for service or work start (whichever earlier) to restoration to service condition, including withdrawal/return time if applicable
- DT(CM) = Down Time due to Corrective Maintenance (hours) - Total downtime for corrective maintenance, counted from train becoming unfit for service or work start (whichever earlier) to restoration to service condition, including return to revenue service time if applicable, excluding unreasonable delays not attributable to rolling stock
- Target: ≥95%
- Assessment: After 18 months from start of revenue operation, calculated monthly over preceding 6 months
- Stabilization Period: 6 months after putting train into revenue service before counting for reliability calculations

**MDBF (Mean Distance Between Failure):**
```
MDBF = Total Operating Distance of Available Fleet / Total Number of Service Failures
```
- Target for 6-Car Fleet:
  - After 6 months of revenue service: ≥60,000 km
  - After 12 months of revenue service: ≥100,000 km
- Calculation based on Service Failures only (relevant failures causing non-availability, withdrawal, or delay ≥3 minutes)

**MDBCF (Mean Distance Between Component Failures):**
```
MDBCF = Total Operating Distance of Fleet / Total Service Failures of Identical Items
```
- System-specific targets (Unit: 3-car train-km):
  - Propulsion System: 500,000 km
    - Shoe collectors: 5,300,000 km
    - HSCB and Earthing Switch: 1,350,000 km
    - Traction Inverter: 1,350,000 km
    - Traction Motor: 7,450,000 km
  - Auxiliary Supply System: 1,050,000 km
    - Auxiliary Converter: 1,800,000 km
    - Battery Charger: 3,400,000 km
    - Back-up Battery: 8,950,000 km
  - Air Supply and Friction Brake Equipment: 700,000 km
  - Door System and Controls: 1,000,000 km
  - VAC System: 800,000 km
  - Communication System: 1,450,000 km
  - Couplers and Draft Gear: 6,952,000 km
    - Automatic Couplers: 12,400,000 km
    - Semi Permanent Coupler: 15,900,000 km
  - Bogies: 3,650,000 km
    - Drive gear and coupling: 8,300,000 km
    - Primary Suspension: 12,500,000 km
    - Secondary Suspension: 13,600,000 km
  - Lighting System: 7,450,000 km
  - TCMS: 2,500,000 km

**MTTR (Mean Time To Repair):**
```
MTTR = Cumulative Time (including access time) / Total Number of Relevant Failures
```
- System-specific targets (Unit: Hours):
  - Propulsion System: 2.5 hours
    - Third rail current collector: 1.5 hours
    - HSCB and Earthing Switch: 2.5 hours
    - Power converter - Inverter: 3.0 hours
    - Traction Motor: 3.0 hours
  - Auxiliary Supply System: 1.7 hours
    - Auxiliary Converter: 2.0 hours
    - Battery Charger: 2.0 hours
    - Back-up Battery: 1.0 hour
  - Air Supply and Friction Brake Equipment: 2.0 hours
  - Door System and Controls: 1.2 hours
  - VAC System: 1.5 hours
  - Communication System: 1.2 hours
  - Couplers and Draft Gear: 2.2 hours
    - Automatic Couplers: 2.2 hours
    - Semi Permanent Coupler: 2.0 hours
  - Bogies: 2.0 hours
    - Drive gear and coupling: 2.0 hours
    - Primary Suspension: 2.0 hours
    - Mechanical Parts & Components: 2.0 hours
    - Secondary Suspension: 2.0 hours
  - Lighting System: 1.0 hour
  - TCMS: 1.5 hours
  - Gangway: 1.0 hour
- LRU replacement target: <30 minutes

**Pattern Failure Identification:**
A pattern failure occurs when (as per RAMS Plan Section 19.2.6(iv)):
- 3 or more relevant service failures of same replaceable part/item/equipment occur in same manner in identical or equivalent applications at rate ≥20% higher than predicted failure rate, OR
- ≥20% of same replaceable part/item/equipment in fleet has relevant failure in same manner during 18-month moving window (from reliability demonstration start to end of Defect Liability Period)
- Identification logic: Track component-level failures (not system-level), compare actual failure rate vs predicted rate, flag items meeting either criterion above

#### 2.5.2 Report Generation
- **Availability Report**: Calculate based on DT(OPM) and DT(CM) extracted from job cards (Duration of Repair field, CM/PM/OPM classification), apply formula per Section 8.2, display monthly trend over preceding 6 months, highlight if <95%
- **MDBF Report**: Fleet-level calculation using total KM from Fleet Management module / total Service Failures from job cards (where Withdraw=Yes OR Delay=Yes with Delay≥3min OR Effects on Train Service=Yes), compare against targets (60,000 km @ 6 months, 100,000 km @ 12 months)
- **MDBCF Report**: Component-level calculation by system, using total fleet KM / service failures of identical items, compare against Table 2 targets, display per system/equipment hierarchy
- **MTTR Report**: Calculate per system/equipment using sum of Duration of Repair (hr) / count of relevant failures, compare against Table 3 targets, highlight items exceeding target MTTR
- **Pattern Failure Report**: Identify components with ≥3 failures in same manner, calculate actual failure rate, compare with predicted rate (from Reliability Prediction data), flag items with rate ≥20% higher OR ≥20% fleet penetration in 18-month window, display clickable list with full job card details
- **Monthly Reliability Demonstration Report**: Comprehensive report covering MDBF, MDBCF, MTTR, Availability, Pattern Failures, corrective actions taken, submitted to Engineer monthly

## 6. RAMS Compliance Requirements (Per RAMS Plan DRCA No. RS(3R)/PP/F/B864/A1)

### 6.1 Reliability Targets
- Fleet MDBF: 60,000 km (6 months), 100,000 km (12 months) for 6-car fleet
- Component MDBCF: As per Table 2 in Section 2.5.1
- Demonstration period: 6 months stabilization + monitoring until end of Defect Liability Period
- Monitoring scope: All relevant failures during revenue service, simulated revenue operations, pre-departure checkouts

### 6.2 Availability Target
- Minimum 95% availability
- Assessment: After 18 months from start of revenue operation, calculated monthly over preceding 6 months
- Formula: [1 - (DT(OPM) + DT(CM)) / Total Time] × 100
- Corrective action: If target not achieved, continue monthly assessment until achieved; contractor takes necessary action at own expense

### 6.3 Maintainability Targets
- LRU replacement: <30 minutes
- System-specific MTTR as per Table 3 in Section 2.5.1
- Maintainability demonstration: Complete within 6 months after delivery of first train

### 6.4 Pattern Failure Criteria
- ≥3 relevant service failures of same part in same manner
- Failure rate ≥20% higher than predicted, OR
- ≥20% of fleet has same failure during 18-month window
- Corrective action: Design change or software modification at contractor's expense

## 7. Failure Classification (Per RAMS Plan Section 7.2)

### 7.1 Relevant Failure
- Fault within design/environmental specification limits
- Improper operation due to contractor-supplied documentation
- Transient failures including No Fault Found (if attributable to rolling stock per Engineer's opinion)

### 7.2 Non-Relevant Failure
- Caused by non-contractor equipment malfunction
- Human error (except documentation-related)
- Accidents not associated with normal operation
- Operation outside design/environmental limits

### 7.3 Service Failure
Relevant failure(s) during revenue service causing:
- Non-availability to start revenue service after pre-departure checkout
- Withdrawal from revenue service
- Delay ≥3 minutes from schedule/timetable at destination station for one-way trip

## 14. RAMS Calculation Implementation Details

### 14.1 Data Collection from Job Cards
- Extract for Availability calculation:
  - Duration of Repair (hr) field
  - CM/PM/OPM classification
  - Job Card Issued Date/Time
  - Job Card Close Date/Time
  - Calculate downtime = Close DateTime - Issued DateTime
  - Categorize as DT(CM) if CM, DT(OPM) if OPM
  - Exclude PM service checks from DT(OPM)

- Extract for MDBF calculation:
  - Train Odometer Reading Data (kms)
  - Service Failure flag: Withdraw=Yes OR (Delay=Yes AND Delay Time≥3min) OR Effects on Train Service=Yes
  - Count only Service Failures for denominator

- Extract for MDBCF calculation:
  - Component/Equipment/Part(s) fields
  - Train Odometer Reading Data (kms)
  - Service Failure flag (same as MDBF)
  - Group by identical items (Component + Equipment + System)

- Extract for MTTR calculation:
  - Duration of Repair (hr) field
  - System/Sub-System/Equipment fields
  - Relevant Failure flag (exclude Non-Relevant per Section 7.2)

- Extract for Pattern Failure:
  - Component/Equipment/Part(s) fields
  - Failure Name field
  - Count failures per identical item in same manner
  - Compare with predicted failure rate (from Reliability Prediction database)
  - Calculate fleet penetration percentage

### 14.2 Calculation Triggers
- Real-time recalculation when:
  - New job card added/updated
  - Job card closed
  - KM data updated
  - Assessment period changed
- Monthly automated report generation

### 14.3 Predicted Failure Rate Database
- Store predicted failure rates for all components/systems from Reliability Apportionment and Prediction Report
- Use for Pattern Failure comparison
- Update when design changes occur

## 17. Critical Implementation Notes

### 17.1 RAMS Calculation Accuracy
- All calculations must strictly follow RAMS Plan document definitions
- Availability: Use DT(OPM) and DT(CM) as defined in Section 19.4.1, not simple downtime sum
- MDBF: Use Service Failures only (withdrawal/delay≥3min/non-availability), not all relevant failures
- MDBCF: Calculate per identical item population, not per system
- Pattern Failure: Identify at component level with rate comparison, not system level
- MTTR: Include access time, calculate per system/equipment per Table 3

### 17.2 Data Integrity
- Job Card and NCR auto-sync based on exact match of TrainSet + Car + Item Name + Serial No. (optional)
- KM data consistency: Use Train Odometer Reading from job cards, supplement with manual daily entry
- Failure classification: Implement logic to distinguish Relevant/Non-Relevant/Service Failures per definitions

- Validate all calculations against RAMS Plan Annexures

- 6-month stabilization period before counting train for reliability calculations
